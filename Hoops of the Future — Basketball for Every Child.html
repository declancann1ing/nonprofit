    Empowering Kids on the Autism Spectrum Through the Love of Basketball

    Hoops of the Future is an IRS-recognized 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to teaching children on the autism spectrum the joy, teamwork, and confidence that come from playing basketball. Proudly partnered with Autism Tree to further our mission.

    Our Mission

    Building futures, one hoop at a time

    "Hoops of the Future empowers kids on the autism spectrum with an equal chance to learn and enjoy the game of basketball."

    As an IRS-recognized 501(c)(3) organization, we create adaptive basketball programs that nurture physical, social, and emotional growth for children on the autism spectrum — in partnership with Autism Tree.

    Inclusivity

    We believe every child on the autism spectrum deserves access to sports and the community it builds.

    Teamwork

    Basketball teaches cooperation, communication, and trust — skills that last a lifetime.

    Confidence

    Through practice and play, our kids discover strengths they never knew they had.

    Our Program

    Designed for kids on the autism spectrum

    Our adaptive basketball program is built to meet each child where they are, fostering growth in a safe and inclusive setting.

    About Us

    Meet the team behind the mission

    We're a passionate group of individuals committed to making basketball accessible to every child.

    Pranav Mahendran

    Founder & CEO

    Hi! I'm Pranav, the Founder of Hoops of the Future. I played basketball at a national level for over 6 years and hope to bring the joy I experienced with the sport to kids all over San Diego. I was the president of a chess club for kids as well, and created Hoops of the Future in order to give kids on the autism spectrum an equal opportunity to love the sport I played growing up.

    Samyag Singhai

    Co-Founder & Treasurer

    Hi, my name is Samyag and I am the Co-Founder of Hoops of the Future. I go to Torrey Pines High school and I have played basketball for almost 5 years. Whether it is watching or playing basketball, I have a passion for the sport and want to give every kid out there a chance to learn and play the game of basketball. As a assistant coach for youth basketball, I hope to teach all kids on how to play basketball.

    Declan Canning

I think everyone should be to try new things and I am proud to be able to teach and give the opportunity to play basketball to kids on the autism spectrum.
